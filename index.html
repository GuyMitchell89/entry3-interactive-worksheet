Apostrophes & Homophones (Interactive Worksheet)

Part 1: Apostrophes

Use apostrophes for: Contractions (missing letters: don’t = do not) and Possession (belongs to: Sarah’s book).
Examples: It’s raining (It is); The dog’s tail (tail of the dog). Avoid using apostrophes for plurals.

Teacher Tips

  • Highlight its (possessive, no apostrophe) vs it’s (it is).
  • For names ending in s, many styles accept James’s. Entry 3: be consistent.
  • Encourage reading aloud to check contractions.

Task 1: Add the Missing Apostrophes

Rewrite each sentence correctly.

Think: Is it a contraction (it is, cannot, let us, who is) or possession (dog’s tail)?

Task 2: Contractions

Match the words to their contractions. Choose the correct option for each.

Common contractions: do not → don’t; they are → they’re; you will → you’ll; we have → we’ve; it is → it’s.

—

Task 3: Possession

Add an apostrophe to show possession.

For plural nouns already ending in s (e.g., children is irregular), place the apostrophe after the noun appropriately.

—

Part 2: Homophones

Homophones sound the same but have different spellings and meanings. Examples: there / their / they’re, your / you’re, its / it’s.

Spot the Difference

  • there (place), their (belonging), they’re (they are).
  • your (belonging), you’re (you are).
  • its (belonging), it’s (it is).

Task 4: Choose the Correct Word

Select the correct homophone for each sentence.

Think about meaning: place vs possession; you are vs your; number vs too much vs direction.

—

Task 5: Fill in the Blanks

Use the correct homophone.

Check each: belonging (their), place (there), contraction of they are (they’re).

—

Challenge

Write three sentences: one with possession, one with a contraction, and one with a homophone.
